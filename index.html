Mixing science
pH Compatibility
Each peptide is formulated within a specific pH range. Mixing peptides with different pH requirements can destabilize one or both, compromising effectiveness.
Solubility Differences
Incompatible solubilities can cause clumping or sedimentation — one peptide may fail to dissolve and lose effectiveness.
Chemical Modifications
Acetylated, PEGylated, or otherwise modified peptides may not mix with unmodified ones — can cause aggregation or reduced bioavailability.
⚠ Always consult your provider before combining peptides. This is not medical advice.

What are peptides?
Peptides are strings of amino acids — the building blocks of proteins. The body produces them naturally (endogenous), but they can also be administered externally (exogenous) to support specific biological functions.
20 amino acids Thousands of sequences Diverse applications
Routes of Administration
Subcutaneous injectionMost common
Why injectable?Peptides degrade in digestive tract. Bioavailability oral = 1-2%
Exceptions (oral/topical)Collagen (63% oral), GHK-Cu (topical)
Storage (reconstituted)Fridge at ~4°C / 39°F, up to 28 days
Needle tipThin gauge — much less discomfort than a standard shot
Never shakeShaking degrades the peptide — swirl gently only

Safety considerations
Immunogenicity
Synthetic peptides can trigger immune responses. At worst, antibodies can neutralize the peptide or cause systemic reactions. ISR (injection site reactions) are the most common mild form.
Carcinogenicity Risk
GH secretagogues (Sermorelin, Tesamorelin, CJC-1295) are contraindicated for anyone with active cancer or a personal/family history of cancer — their growth-promoting effects can accelerate tumor growth.
Generally Safe Profile
Pharmaceutical-grade peptides are generally well-tolerated. For most people, adverse events are limited to mild, transient flu-like effects and injection site reactions.
Legal status (US)
Peptide legality in the US is governed by Section 503A of the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act. Compounded peptides must come from accredited pharmacies and either derive from an FDA-approved drug or appear on the 503A bulks list.
Currently in legal gray area (not on 503A bulks list):
BPC-157 CJC-1295 Ipamorelin MOTS-C GHK-Cu (injectable) Selank TA1 TB-500 Epitalon
⚠ FDA is not actively enforcing prohibition during evaluation, and 503A exemptions exist with a valid prescription. The bulks list is a living document — status can change.
How peptides are made
Bioactive (natural)From plants/animals. Enzymatic hydrolysis or fermentation. Collagen, GHK-Cu.
SyntheticLab-derived amino acid chains. Semaglutide, Tirzepatide, Tesamorelin. Up to ~50 AAs.
RecombinantRecombinant DNA technology. Can exceed 50 AAs. Higher upfront cost, better at scale.
Research-grade warningPurity as low as 60%. Not for human use. Only pharmaceutical-grade for therapy.
